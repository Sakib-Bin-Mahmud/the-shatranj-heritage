# **2. Executive Summary**

## **Background**

Chess has experienced sustained growth in Bangladesh over the past decade, driven by increased participation in schools, universities, chess academies, clubs, and online platforms. The accessibility of online learning resources and the popularity of international chess tournaments have further expanded interest across different age groups.

Despite this growth, the commercial ecosystem surrounding chess remains fragmented. Enthusiasts typically purchase equipment through:

* General-purpose marketplaces  
* Social media sellers  
* Small local retailers  
* Informal importers  
* Overseas websites

These channels often provide inconsistent product quality, limited selection, uncertain authenticity, and little specialized guidance.

At the same time, Bangladesh has a rich tradition of craftsmanship. Artisans produce handmade wooden, terracotta, bamboo, brass, and marble products that could be adapted into unique chess merchandise. However, these artisans often lack the digital infrastructure and market access needed to reach a wider audience.

This creates an opportunity to establish a specialized platform that not only serves chess enthusiasts but also promotes local craftsmanship and strengthens the chess community.

---

## **Business Opportunity**

The Shatranj Heritage aims to become the first dedicated chess commerce platform in Bangladesh by combining:

* Specialized product curation  
* Trusted customer experience  
* Artisan empowerment  
* Community engagement  
* Scalable digital commerce

Rather than competing with large marketplaces solely on product breadth or price, The Shatranj Heritage will differentiate itself through expertise, authenticity, and a strong focus on the chess ecosystem.

---

## **Product Vision**

The Shatranj Heritage is envisioned as more than an online store. It will evolve into a comprehensive platform that connects:

* Chess players  
* Collectors  
* Schools  
* Universities  
* Chess clubs  
* Coaches  
* Tournament organizers  
* Local artisans  
* Equipment suppliers

The platform will support the growth of chess culture while creating sustainable business opportunities for local manufacturers and artisans.

---

## **Long-Term Aspiration**

Over time, The Shatranj Heritage seeks to become the leading chess commerce and community platform in South Asia, expanding beyond Bangladesh while preserving a strong focus on regional craftsmanship and local communities.

The following principles will guide business decisions, product prioritization, and operational strategy throughout the lifecycle of The Shatranj Heritage:

1. **Customer-Centricity**: Every feature and process should simplify the buying experience and deliver value to chess enthusiasts.  
2. **Quality Over Quantity**: Prioritize curated, authentic, and durable products rather than maximizing catalog size.  
3. **Support Local Craftsmanship**: Actively promote and invest in products created by local artisans and manufacturers.  
4. **Community First**: Strengthen the chess ecosystem by partnering with clubs, schools, academies, and tournament organizers.  
5. **Scalable Foundation**: Build business processes and technology that support future growth into a multi-vendor marketplace and regional expansion.  
6. **Data-Driven Decisions**: Use customer behavior, sales metrics, and market insights to guide product evolution and business strategy.

## **Review Notes**

Part 2 defines **what the business must be able to accomplish** to deliver on The Shatranj Heritage's vision. These requirements will be traced to functional and non-functional requirements in the SRS, ensuring every software feature directly supports a documented business need.

### **Recommendations to strengthen the business model**

Given The Shatranj Heritage's vision of becoming more than a typical e-commerce store, I'd recommend adding a few strategic business capabilities—even if they're not part of the MVP:

* **Artisan Storytelling**: Each locally crafted product should include the artisan's profile, region, craftsmanship details, and production story to reinforce authenticity and differentiate the platform.  
* **Institutional Procurement**: Introduce business workflows for schools, universities, clubs, and tournament organizers, including quotation requests, bulk pricing, and purchase approvals.  
* **Custom Orders**: Support configurable products (e.g., engraved boards, personalized pieces, club logos) through a quotation and design approval process rather than a standard checkout.  
* **Tournament Packages**: Allow organizations to purchase curated bundles (boards, clocks, scoresheets, demo boards, trophies) tailored to event size and competition level.  
* **Pre-orders for Limited Collections**: Enable demand validation for artisan-made or seasonal products before production begins, reducing inventory risk and supporting small-scale makers.

These capabilities align closely with The Shatranj Heritage's long-term vision and will provide a stronger competitive moat than simply offering a larger catalog.

# Part 4: Non-Functional Business Requirements (NFR)

## **1. Purpose**

This section defines the **non-functional business requirements** for The Shatranj Heritage. These requirements describe the quality attributes, operational expectations, compliance obligations, and business constraints that the platform must satisfy to ensure a reliable, secure, scalable, and maintainable service.

Unlike functional requirements, which define **what** the platform should do, non-functional requirements define **how well** it should perform.

These business-level NFRs will later be refined into measurable technical specifications in the Software Requirements Specification (SRS).
