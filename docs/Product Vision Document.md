# **1. Executive Summary**

Chess has experienced significant growth in Bangladesh over the past decade, fueled by increased participation in schools, universities, clubs, online platforms, and international tournaments. Despite this growing interest, enthusiasts face a fragmented purchasing experience. Chess products are scattered across general e-commerce platforms, imported through informal channels, or available only in a handful of physical stores with limited selection.

There is currently no dedicated digital platform in Bangladesh that serves as the primary destination for chess equipment, books, handcrafted products, tournament supplies, and community engagement.

This product aims to fill that gap by creating the country's first niche e-commerce platform focused entirely on chess. Beyond selling products, the platform will celebrate local craftsmanship, support artisans, strengthen the chess community, and eventually become the leading chess commerce ecosystem across South Asia.

# **2. Problem Statement**

## **Current Challenges**

### **Customers**

Chess players often struggle to:

* Find quality chess products in one place.  
* Verify the authenticity of imported equipment.  
* Purchase tournament-grade chess boards and clocks.  
* Access locally crafted premium chess sets.  
* Compare products effectively.  
* Purchase customized chess merchandise.  
* Obtain reliable after-sales support.  
* Discover new chess-related products.

---

### **Local Artisans**

Traditional craftsmen producing wooden, terracotta, bamboo, brass, marble, or handcrafted chess sets face several challenges:

* Limited online visibility.  
* Dependence on local fairs or exhibitions.  
* Lack of digital marketing.  
* No nationwide distribution.  
* Difficulty reaching international buyers.  
* Inconsistent demand.

---

### **Chess Organizations**

Chess clubs, academies, schools, and tournament organizers often need to source equipment from multiple vendors, making procurement time-consuming and inefficient.

---

### **Existing Market**

General marketplaces such as Daraz prioritize breadth over specialization. Chess products are:

* Difficult to discover.  
* Mixed with unrelated items.  
* Poorly categorized.  
* Often low quality.  
* Rarely accompanied by expert guidance.

The lack of specialization limits the overall customer experience.

# **3. Opportunity Statement**

Bangladesh has a growing chess ecosystem, but its commercial infrastructure has not evolved alongside it.

This creates an opportunity to build a specialized platform that combines:

* Commerce  
* Community  
* Craftsmanship  
* Education  
* Tournament support

into one cohesive ecosystem.

Rather than competing solely on price, the platform can compete on expertise, curation, trust, and cultural relevance.

# **14. Long-Term Vision**

Within five years, the platform aims to evolve from a niche e-commerce website into the leading chess ecosystem in South Asia. Success will be measured not only by revenue, but also by the strength of the community it fosters, the opportunities it creates for local artisans, and its contribution to the growth of chess culture in the region.

The ultimate aspiration is to ensure that when anyone in Bangladesh—or eventually across South Asia—thinks of chess products, learning resources, tournaments, or handcrafted chess artistry, this platform is the first destination that comes to mind.
